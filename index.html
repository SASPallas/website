The problem

Valuable data exists. Safe external collaboration does not.

Government and regulated organizations have datasets that could dramatically improve AI systems, but they often cannot safely share that data with external vendors. The result is weaker model development, slower procurement, and less competition.

🧪

Vendors train on inferior, stale data

Without controlled access to real operational datasets, ML vendors often train on synthetic, incomplete, or lower-value data.

📏

Procurement lacks objective testing

AI vendors are too often selected through proposals and demos instead of measurable performance on hidden benchmark datasets.

🏢

Incumbents stay entrenched

When new entrants cannot access secure evaluation environments, agencies remain dependent on large established contractors by default.

⏳

Innovation becomes slow and expensive

Without a trusted AI sandbox, pilots take longer, comparisons are inconsistent, and teams spend more to get less meaningful progress.

Platform overview

Kaggle for regulated industries — built for real procurement.

Pallas Exchange is a secure AI evaluation and data-sharing platform that combines trusted sandboxed data access, controlled model competitions, automated evaluation, and procurement decision support. It is designed for high-stakes environments where privacy, security, and traceability matter.

How it works

Run secure ML vendor competitions from dataset ingestion to shortlist.

We offer a challenge platform for regulated organizations to evaluate third-party AI models against hidden datasets, with governed access, reproducible scoring, and procurement-ready performance evidence.

1

Upload datasets into a secure compute environment

Store sensitive data inside a protected environment with version control, privacy protections, access governance, and full activity logging.

2

Define an AI evaluation challenge

Define the task, scoring criteria, approved submission types, and competition timeline for vendors participating in the sandbox.

3

Run secure model evaluations

Developers submit models that run inside the sandbox without accessing the raw dataset.

4

Benchmark vendors and support procurement

Automated scoring generates leaderboards, validation artifacts, and procurement-ready evaluation reports.
